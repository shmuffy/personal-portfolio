## Overview

Front sensor hub for the Highlander Racing Formula SAE Electric vehicle. The board aggregates all front-end vehicle dynamics sensors into a single CAN node, reducing harness complexity and providing clean, filtered telemetry data to the Vehicle Control Unit at race-event reliability levels.

## Sensor Integration

The hub interfaces to:
- **Throttle position potentiometer** (redundant dual-channel, APPS plausibility check per FSAE rules)
- **Brake pressure sensor** (front circuit)
- **Steering angle potentiometer**
- **Shock potentiometers** (front left / front right suspension travel)
- **Thermistors** (brake caliper thermal monitoring)

Each analog input passes through a band-tuned RC filter. Filter cutoff frequencies range from **6 Hz** (suspension sensors — reject vibration above sprung frequency) to **515 Hz** (brake pressure — preserve transient response through braking events). Filter design was validated against measured chassis vibration spectra to avoid aliasing.

## Signal Chain

The STM32F405 internal ADC samples all sensor channels at 12-bit resolution. Firmware applies offset calibration, gain correction, and rate-of-change monitoring per channel. The APPS (accelerator pedal position sensor) channel implements the FSAE-required implausibility detection with a 100 ms timeout and throttle cut on fault.

## CAN Architecture

Dual CAN transceivers provide node redundancy. Each transceiver uses 60 Ω split termination — two 120 Ω resistors in series with a bypass capacitor to ground at the midpoint — improving common-mode noise rejection on the vehicle harness. EMI and ESD protection is placed at the board edge before the transceiver inputs.

## Mechanical Integration

All connectors are sealed Deutsch DT or Ampseal series, rated for the vibration and moisture environment of an open-wheel race car. Connector placement was coordinated with the harness routing to minimize harness length and avoid routing signal cables near high-current inverter cables.
