## Overview

Senior design project comprising two interdependent hardware systems: a 300 W programmable electronic load for characterizing battery packs under controlled discharge conditions, and a 10-cell Li-ion BMS providing hardware-enforced protection for the pack under test.

## Programmable Electronic Load

### Power Stage

Multiple IRFP250N MOSFETs are configured as linear pass elements in parallel, each sourced by a precision shunt resistor for current sharing and Kelvin-sensed current measurement. Linear operation (vs. switching) eliminates ripple current injection into the battery under test, which would contaminate impedance measurements.

### Control Loop

A **TL431**-stabilized analog feedback loop regulates drain current against a DAC-set reference. The STM32F3 generates the current setpoint via DAC and monitors the sensed shunt voltage via ADC, implementing a digital outer loop on top of the analog inner loop. This two-tier architecture gives fast transient response (analog inner loop bandwidth ~10 kHz) with programmable precision (12-bit DAC, ~5 mA resolution at 20 A full scale).

### Thermal Management

Thermal derating is implemented in firmware: as heatsink temperature rises (measured by NTC on the MOSFET mounting surface), the firmware reduces the maximum allowable current setpoint proportionally. Fan PWM speed is controlled by a second STM32 timer channel, scaled to heatsink temperature.

An iso-trigger input allows external instruments (oscilloscope, data logger) to synchronously trigger load step events without ground loop interference.

## 10-Cell Li-ion BMS

### Protection Architecture

The **BQ76930** cell monitor IC handles cell voltage measurement across all 10 cells and enforces hardware overcurrent and short-circuit protection via the MOSFET gate drivers built into the IC. External 55 V rated high-side MOSFET breakers implement the charge and discharge isolation paths.

Protection thresholds:
- **OC (sustained):** 50 A
- **SC (transient):** 60 A with ~100 µs response
- **NTC thermal cutoff:** ~65 °C (passive thermistor latch)

A 20 mΩ / 7 W precision shunt in the main current path provides Kelvin-sensed current measurement for the cell monitor's coulomb counting.

### Power Tree

A regulated **12 V → 3.3 V** converter provides the BMS digital rail. The 12 V intermediate rail is derived from the pack with a buck converter, allowing BMS self-power from the pack being protected. This eliminates external power requirements during standalone operation.

Passive cell balancing bleeds excess energy from high-SOC cells through switched resistors, maintaining cell-to-cell voltage spread below 20 mV at end-of-charge.
