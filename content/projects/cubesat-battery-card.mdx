## Overview

Battery management card designed for the C.U.T.I.E. (CubeSat Unit for Terrestrial and Interplanetary Exploration) payload as part of the Highlander Space Program's liquid propulsion rocketry club. The card manages a 4S Li-ion pack and delivers regulated power to the CITRUS onboard computer and RF telemetry subsystem.

## Architecture

The design integrates three Texas Instruments ICs in a coordinated power path:

- **BQ77915** — front-end protection IC handling overvoltage, undervoltage, overcurrent, and short-circuit events at the cell level. MOSFET-based charge/discharge isolation switches cut the pack from load or charger under fault conditions.
- **BQ34Z100** — fuel gauge IC providing state-of-charge estimation, remaining capacity, and NTC-based temperature monitoring. Communicates pack state over I²C to the flight computer.
- **BQ25713** — buck-boost charger and power-path controller. Manages the transition between charging and discharge, regulates the system bus, and allows simultaneous charging and load operation without a glitch on the output rails.

Filtered voltage, current, and NTC sensing feeds each IC independently. Passive cell balancing is implemented via switched resistors across individual cells to equalize state-of-charge during top-balance.

## Output Regulation

Protected 5 V and 3.3 V rails are derived from the regulated system bus and distributed to the CITRUS OBC and the RF downlink subsystem. Rail sequencing ensures the flight computer powers up before the RF system draws current.

## Design Considerations

CubeSat form factor imposed tight area constraints. Component placement prioritized thermal management for the BQ25713's switching node, keeping it away from the analog sensing circuitry. Differential sensing lines are routed with matched lengths and common-mode filtering to reject switching noise from the charger stage.
