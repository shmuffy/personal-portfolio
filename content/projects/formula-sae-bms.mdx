## Overview

Battery Management System PCB designed for Highlander Racing's Formula SAE Electric vehicle, managing a 400 V traction pack under race conditions. The board serves as the electrical safety backbone of the car — monitoring cell health, enforcing protection thresholds, and communicating pack state to the Vehicle Control Unit over CAN.

## Architecture

The STM32 host processor runs the BMS application logic: polling cell voltages and temperatures, enforcing state-of-charge limits, and commanding contactors. Communication to the high-voltage domain crosses a galvanic isolation barrier, separating the low-voltage control circuitry from the traction pack potential.

The **BQ76PLQ1** stacked cell monitor interfaces to the cell stack via a daisy-chain topology, enabling measurement of individual cell voltages across the full pack without requiring high-common-mode ADC inputs on the host side.

DC-DC power stages provide isolated supplies for both sides of the isolation barrier, ensuring the MCU rails and cell monitor rails are independently regulated and fault-tolerant.

## CAN Integration

The BMS communicates with the VCU and inverter over CAN bus, broadcasting pack voltage, current, state-of-charge, fault status, and thermal data at configurable intervals. The CAN node implements the FSAE-required safety messaging protocol, including heartbeat monitoring and fault escalation.

## Validation

Electrical validation covered:
- Isolation integrity across the galvanic barrier under DC and AC stimulus
- Contactor coil characterization (inrush, drop-out, coil resistance at temperature)
- CAN signal integrity at 1 Mbit/s under inverter-induced EMI conditions
- Current sensor accuracy (zero offset, gain error, thermal drift)
- Thermal performance under sustained transient race-load current profiles

PCB layouts revised to meet IPC-2221B creepage/clearance requirements at 400 V, with Gerbers released for manufacturing.
